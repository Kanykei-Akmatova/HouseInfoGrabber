Greely

Part of the Ottawa - Gatineau metropolitan area, Greely is a neighbourhood within Ottawa, Ontario.

Transportation

Cars are an excellent transportation option in Greely. Parking is generally easy. In contrast, the public transit system in Greely is not very convenient. Nonetheless, homeowners benefit from a few bus lines, and bus stops are usually within walking distance. Commuting by bicycle is sometimes challenging in Greely because there are few bike lanes, and there are a good number of elevation changes. Travelling on foot is also not very practical for property owners in Greely because meeting daily needs is very inconvenient by walking.

Services

There are only a few primary schools in this part of the city and therefore they can be a rather long walk away. In addition, Greely does not contain any high schools. Furthermore, parents and their children may find it very hard to reach daycares as a pedestrian. As far as food is concerned, it is very rarely an option for house buyers in Greely to buy groceries on foot.

Character

Greely is very good for those who enjoy a quiet atmosphere, as noise from streets and other parts of the city is rarely an issue. It is rather difficult to reach green spaces in the neighbourhood since they aren't well-situated. Despite that, there are about 10 parks nearby for residents to check out.

Housing

Single detached homes are the most common housing type, representing the vast majority of dwellings in Greely, while townhouses are the main alternative in the housing stock. This part of Ottawa experienced its largest construction boom after the year 2000, so most of the available properties are from this era. This neighbourhood offers mainly three bedroom and four or more bedroom homes. Nearly 95% of the dwellings in the neighbourhood are occupied by homeowners and 5% are rented.
